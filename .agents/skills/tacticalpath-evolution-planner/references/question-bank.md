# TacticalPath Planning Question Bank

Use this bank to drive deep discovery. Do not ask all of these at once. Ask the next best 5 to 8 questions based on what remains unclear.

## 1. Product Thesis

- What is TacticalPath really trying to become in one sentence?
- If you removed all the fancy UI, what is the hardest problem this product solves?
- Why does this need to exist now?
- What is the emotional promise to the user: confidence, improvement, identity, accountability, status, coaching access, something else?
- What should users feel after 7 days?
- What should users feel after 90 days?
- What do you explicitly not want this to become?
- If the app succeeds wildly, what category will people place it in?
- If users describe it incorrectly, what misunderstanding do you expect?

## 2. Ideal User

- Who is the first paying user?
- What rating band are they in?
- Are they beginner, club player, serious improver, coach, scholastic parent, academy owner, or tournament grinder?
- What device do they primarily use?
- How much time do they realistically train per day?
- What tools do they use today?
- What frustrates them about current chess products?
- What is their budget tolerance monthly and yearly?
- What would make them trust this product enough to keep returning?

## 3. User Segments and Expansion

- Is the first product for one segment only or multiple segments?
- Which segment should be ignored in v1?
- Could coaches become a separate customer class later?
- Could clubs, schools, or academies become B2B customers?
- Could creators or titled players become content partners?
- Do you ever want parents managing junior accounts?
- Do you ever want team, school, or academy dashboards?
- Do you want a product that remains solo-first, or one that grows into a network?

## 4. Core Loop

- What is the main habit loop?
- What should a user open the app to do most often?
- What is the shortest path from app open to meaningful value?
- What is the daily loop?
- What is the weekly loop?
- What is the comeback loop after a loss streak?
- What keeps users returning if they are not buying coaching?
- What keeps users returning if they are not actively playing rated games?
- Which loop matters more: analysis, puzzles, study plan, or coach feedback?

## 5. Onboarding

- What do you need to know about a user before giving value?
- Should onboarding ask rating, platform, goals, openings, time control, or learning style?
- What is the minimum onboarding that still allows personalization?
- Should users be able to skip onboarding?
- How do you avoid boring users before first value?
- What is the first impressive moment in the product?
- What should happen in the first 3 minutes?
- What should happen in the first session if the user has no PGN to upload?

## 6. Game Analysis

- What does "analysis" mean in your product?
- Is it engine accuracy only, coaching explanation, pattern extraction, training prescription, or all of them?
- Should analysis work from pasted PGN, imported game history, screenshots, live boards, or all of the above?
- Which input method matters first?
- How trustworthy must the analysis be before launch?
- Do you want Stockfish-grade engine analysis, LLM explanation on top, or a hybrid system?
- Should the analysis be fast and shallow or slow and deep?
- Do users need a single game review, batch review, or recurring trend analysis?
- What are the specific outputs: blunders, missed tactics, opening review, time usage, recurring motifs, study recommendations?
- What should happen after analysis is complete?

## 7. Puzzle System

- Are puzzles a core product pillar or a supporting habit mechanic?
- Should puzzles be manually curated, engine-extracted, AI-generated, or mixed?
- Do you want theme-based training, rating-based training, spaced repetition, or adaptive weakness training?
- What makes your puzzle experience different from Chess.com or Lichess?
- Should puzzle performance feed the study plan automatically?
- Should puzzles be timed, untimed, streak-based, survival-based, or personalized by weakness?
- Do you want daily challenge to be central or peripheral?
- How should hints work?
- Should explanations appear after failure, after success, or always?

## 8. Study Plan

- What does a study plan actually contain?
- Is it a checklist, curriculum, dynamic recommendation system, coach-authored plan, or adaptive progression engine?
- How much of the plan is system-generated versus hand-authored?
- What are the content units: lessons, drills, positions, PGNs, mini-courses, coach notes?
- How should the study plan adapt to new data?
- What inputs should modify it: game analysis, puzzle results, user goals, time availability, coach interventions?
- Should users edit the plan manually?
- Should the plan ever feel strict, or should it feel assistive?
- How do you prevent the plan from becoming a dead to-do list?

## 9. AI Role

- What exact job should AI perform in TacticalPath?
- Where is AI essential versus optional?
- Should AI explain engine findings, create training tasks, simulate coaching, summarize progress, or generate content?
- What should never be delegated to AI?
- What is the acceptable hallucination risk?
- How will users know when output is engine-backed versus model-generated?
- Do you want branded AI personas or a neutral assistant?
- Should AI be invisible infrastructure or a visible product character?
- Which AI moments are worth paying for?

## 10. Coaching Marketplace

- Is human coaching essential to the business or a premium add-on?
- Are coaches internal, vetted partners, open marketplace sellers, or invite-only pros?
- What do coaches sell: sessions, reviews, courses, recurring mentorship, opening packs?
- How is coach quality validated?
- How do users discover the right coach?
- What trust mechanisms are needed: ratings, sample lessons, credentials, response time, guarantees?
- Should coaching happen inside TacticalPath or off-platform?
- Do you want asynchronous coaching, live sessions, or both?
- Is coach tooling a future separate product?

## 11. Monetization

- What is the first revenue path: subscription, coaching commission, one-time courses, or hybrid?
- Which feature should be behind the paywall first?
- What remains free forever?
- What is the free product supposed to prove?
- When should a free user hit the upgrade moment?
- What pricing logic fits your market and user segment?
- Should there be one premium tier or multiple?
- Could you sell a coach plan and a player plan separately later?
- Could analysis credits make more sense than full subscription?

## 12. Progression and Motivation

- What form of progress matters most: rating, consistency, skill mastery, streaks, milestones, coach validation?
- Do you want visible skill trees, mastery maps, leagues, or minimalist progress?
- What motivates the user without becoming gamified fluff?
- How should wins and losses affect the experience?
- Should the app respond differently during confidence slumps?
- What achievements are meaningful versus childish?
- How should progression connect to actual chess improvement rather than vanity metrics?

## 13. Content Strategy

- Will TacticalPath rely on original instructional content?
- Who creates that content?
- What content types matter first?
- How much content must exist before launch?
- Should openings and lessons be broad or focused?
- Do you want evergreen foundations first or niche high-interest lines first?
- How will content stay fresh?
- What can be generated versus what must be authored?

## 14. Design and Beauty

- What should TacticalPath feel like visually: elite, calm, sharp, luxurious, playful, scientific, cinematic?
- What should make it beautiful in a way competitors are not?
- Is the visual identity closer to premium sports training, high-end strategy software, or modern editorial learning?
- How much motion is desirable?
- Should the UI feel coach-like, lab-like, or game-like?
- Do you want a warm or cold emotional tone?
- Should beauty come from typography, space, illustration, board visuals, or progress storytelling?
- Which current screens already feel closest to the intended brand?
- Which current visual choices are misleading about the real product?

## 15. Information Architecture

- Are the current top-level product areas the right ones?
- Should dashboard, analyze, learn, and profile remain the main structure?
- Is coach connect too early as a top-level tab?
- Should puzzles live inside learn, or stay as a separate habit loop?
- Should users think in terms of activities, outcomes, or training plans?
- What should the bottom nav prioritize on mobile?
- Which screen should be the emotional home of the product?

## 16. Data Model and State

- What are the primary domain entities?
- Do you expect users, games, analyses, motifs, study plans, drills, lessons, coaches, reviews, subscriptions, and messages?
- Which entities need version history?
- Which user outputs should be immutable snapshots?
- What should be derived versus stored?
- Which pieces of progress need auditability?
- If you later add coaches, what permissions and ownership boundaries appear?
- If you later add teams or academies, what multi-tenant concerns appear?

## 17. Architecture and Future-Proofing

- What must be designed as a stable seam now?
- Does analysis need a background job model later?
- Does the app need to support long-running engine work?
- Will you need queues, caching, or event-driven processing?
- Should content and product logic be separable from presentation from day one?
- What would break if mobile apps are added later?
- What would break if multiplayer or community features are added later?
- What would break if coaches require dashboards and operations tools?
- Which features can stay fake longer, and which need real infrastructure early?

## 18. Trust, Safety, and Quality

- What makes users trust analysis results?
- How do you explain uncertainty?
- How do you avoid overclaiming AI capability?
- How do you handle incorrect advice?
- How do you prevent fake coaches or low-quality content if marketplace features arrive?
- What data privacy concerns matter for user games and private prep?
- Do users need to keep certain analyses private?
- What support expectations will paid users have?

## 19. Analytics and Learning Metrics

- What metrics show the product is working?
- What metrics show the user is improving?
- What metrics show the business is healthy?
- Which leading indicators matter before revenue?
- What events should exist from day one?
- How will you know whether analysis, puzzles, or study plan drives retention?
- What would be a false-positive metric that looks good but means nothing?

## 20. Launch Strategy

- What is the smallest believable version of TacticalPath?
- Which 3 features make the first version worth using?
- What features create complexity without proof of value?
- What should be intentionally absent in v1?
- What should be manually operated before being automated?
- Who are the first 10 users?
- How will you recruit them?
- What feedback loop will you run after launch?

## 21. Future Evolutions

- Could TacticalPath grow into a full training operating system for serious players?
- Could it become a coach infrastructure platform?
- Could it become a school or academy product?
- Could it support live tournament prep and opponent scouting?
- Could it become a creator marketplace?
- Could it expand into openings repertoire management?
- Could it support team analytics, club ladders, or sparring partners?
- Could it evolve into native mobile apps or desktop analysis tools?
- Which of these futures should the architecture allow, and which should it deliberately not optimize for yet?

## 22. Anti-Rewrite Questions

- Which decision, if ignored now, would force a painful rewrite later?
- Which decision feels important now but is actually safe to defer?
- Where are you most likely to overbuild?
- Where are you most likely to under-design?
- Which hidden assumptions are coming from competitor products rather than your own thesis?
- If this becomes 10x bigger than expected, what part of today’s concept collapses first?
